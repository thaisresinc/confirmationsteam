⏰ 8:00AM – 9:00AM
Daily Prep — Set Yourself Up to Win
WHAT HAPPENS
Before you dial a single number: check the CX inbox, review any cancellations and reschedules from overnight, check the Same Day appointment list, and do a calendar cleanup. Log into Zoho CRM and the dialer. Know what you're walking into.
WHY IT MATTERS
The prep hour sets the tone for the entire day. An unreviewed calendar means surprises. An unread CX inbox means missed reschedules. Two minutes on each check saves you thirty minutes of chaos later in the day.
🏆 TOP PERFORMER TIP
"Check the CX inbox and calendar before anything else. If there's an overnight cancellation you missed, that appointment is still sitting in the 1st run queue — and you'll waste a call on it."
CLOSE ✕
🔥
⏰ 9:00AM – 12:00PM
1st Run — Call All Next Day Bookings
WHAT HAPPENS
Call every appointment booked for the next day. If the customer answers: confirm the appointment and update the CRM with notes. If they don't answer: leave a voicemail, mark the appointment as Unconfirmed, and update the CRM. Monitor the CX inbox and action SMS/email responses between calls.
WHY IT MATTERS
The 1st run sets the baseline. Every appointment you confirm now is one less to chase later. Every no-answer needs a voicemail and a CRM note — the 2nd run team picks up exactly where you left off. No note = no handover.
⚡ THE LOGIC
"Don't batch your CRM updates — do them immediately after each call. By the time the 2nd run starts, your notes need to be accurate. The person running the next attempt is relying on you."
CLOSE ✕
📋
⏰ 12:00PM – 2:00PM
2nd Run — Re-Attempt Every Unconfirmed
WHAT HAPPENS
Re-attempt all appointments marked Unconfirmed from the 1st run. Same process: customer answers → confirm + CRM notes. No answer → update CRM notes. Continue monitoring the CX inbox and clearing the dashboard between calls.
WHY IT MATTERS
Every unconfirmed appointment is a potential no-show. The 2nd run is where you recover the ones who missed the first call. Most customers who weren't home at 9AM are reachable by midday — this run converts more than people expect.
🎯 COACHING NOTE
"Don't assume a 1st run no-answer is a dead end. Midday is often the sweet spot — customers are on lunch, more available, more receptive. Your tone on a 2nd attempt should be warmer, not more urgent."
CLOSE ✕
☕
⏰ 2:00PM – 4:00PM
3rd & 4th Run + SMS — Keep Pushing
WHAT HAPPENS
The 3rd run (2–3PM) re-attempts remaining unconfirmed appointments. The 4th run (3–4PM) is the final phone attempt plus the SMS blast to all remaining unconfirmed — a direct message to anyone who hasn't picked up. Continue CX inbox and dashboard monitoring throughout.
WHY IT MATTERS
By the 4th run, most of the day's confirmations are locked. The SMS adds a second channel — some customers don't pick up calls but will respond to a text. Don't skip it. Every confirmed appointment in these runs is one less no-show tomorrow.
📅 REMEMBER
"The SMS blast in the 4th run is not a replacement for the call — it's a backup. Send it after the call attempt, not instead of it. Kaysie will confirm the approved SMS template to use."
CLOSE ✕
📞
⏰ 5:00PM – 6:00PM
5th Run — Final Attempt & Recovery
WHAT HAPPENS
Review all remaining unconfirmed or un-contacted appointments. Complete final contact attempts where appropriate. Ensure every CRM status is accurate and fully updated. Add final notes for outstanding appointments and flag high-priority leads for next-day action.
WHY IT MATTERS
The 5th run is your last chance to protect tomorrow's schedule. An unconfirmed appointment left overnight is a risk. Every attempt you make now — even if it doesn't connect — shows in your outbound call count and protects your KPIs.
🧠 THE STRATEGY
"Don't wind down mentally at 5PM. The 5th run often catches customers who just got home from work — this is one of the highest answer-rate windows of the day. Stay sharp."
CLOSE ✕
🏁
⏰ 6:00PM – 7:00PM
End of Day — Leave No Loose Ends
WHAT HAPPENS
Before you log off: ensure the calendar is fully accurate and up to date, complete a final CX inbox check, and confirm every CRM status is correct. Every appointment must have a status — Confirmed, Unconfirmed, Cancelled, or Rescheduled. No blanks.
WHY IT MATTERS
The Solar Specialist's entire day is built on what you hand them. An inaccurate calendar means a specialist arriving at a cancelled appointment. Your EOD is someone else's morning. Treat it that way.
✅ THE CHECKLIST
Calendar accurate ✓  |  CX inbox cleared ✓  |  All CRM statuses updated ✓. Three things. Every. Single. Day.

THE NON-NEGOTIABLES
Three Rules That Drive Results
01
Prep before you dial
Check the CX inbox and calendar before the 1st run, every day. Not after your first call — before. Surprises in the queue are your responsibility to catch early.
02
Every run matters
All 5 runs must be completed every day — non-negotiable. Missing a run isn't catching up later. It's appointments left uncontacted and a confirm rate that drops below minimum.
03
CRM accuracy is non-negotiable
Every call gets a status. Every status gets a note. The specialist who walks into a home tomorrow is relying on what you update tonight. Sloppy notes are not a small thing.
