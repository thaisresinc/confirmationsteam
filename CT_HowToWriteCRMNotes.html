How to Write
CRM Notes
CRM notes are not admin — they're the handover between you and the specialist. Every field you fill in correctly protects the appointment and sets the specialist up to succeed.
📋 2 note templates 💡 Field-by-field breakdown ✅ Good vs bad examples 📄 Filled examples
WHY THIS MATTERS
Your notes are someone else's morning.

The Solar Specialist's entire day is built on what you put in the CRM. Before they walk into a home or join a Zoom call, they read your notes. What you write — or don't write — directly impacts whether the appointment goes smoothly or falls apart.

🏠
The Specialist relies on you
Before every appointment, the specialist reads your CRM notes to know what to expect — the customer's situation, objections raised, what's been confirmed. Incomplete notes mean a cold start.
🔄
The team picks up where you left off
If you can't reach a customer on Run 2, the Run 3 team needs to know what happened on Run 1. Notes are how that handover works. No notes = wasted calls and frustrated customers.
📊
It protects your KPIs
Incomplete CRM notes contribute to no-shows, cancellations, and missed dual attendance. Everything you fill in properly is one less thing that can go wrong on the day.
THE GOLDEN RULES
Before we look at the templates.
⏱️
UPDATE IMMEDIATELY AFTER EVERY CALL
Don't batch your CRM updates. Update the record right after you hang up — while the call is fresh. Waiting means details get lost or confused with the next call.
📝
BE SPECIFIC — NEVER VAGUE
"Spoke to customer" is not a note. "Confirmed with Sarah — husband Mark will attend. Requested electricity bill, not yet received." is a note. Anyone reading it knows exactly what happened.
✅
EVERY FIELD HAS A PURPOSE
Y/N fields aren't ticking boxes — they're a communication protocol between you and the specialist. If Dual Appointment is blank, the specialist doesn't know whether to expect one person or two.
🚨
OBJECTIONS ALWAYS GET NOTED
If a customer raised a concern — "60–90 minutes is too long", "my husband might not make it" — write it in Additional Notes. The specialist needs to know what they're walking into.

Confirmation Call Notes

CONFIRMATION CALL NOTES
AFTER EVERY CALL
APPOINTMENT CONFIRMATION
Confirmed Appointment Date & Time
Y / N
▾
Why this field exists:
The specialist needs to know the customer verbally confirmed they know when the appointment is — not just that a time was booked. A booking in the calendar doesn't mean the customer remembers it.

✓ Good: Y — confirmed Thursday 2PM
✗ Bad: Y (with no context — which run confirmed it?)
Confirmed All Decision Makers Will Be Present
Y / N
▾
Why this field exists:
If only one decision-maker is coming, the specialist knows before they start that the session may not convert. This field protects dual attendance rates and the specialist's time.

✓ Good: Y — both Sarah and Mark confirmed attending
✗ Bad: N (with no explanation — why not? Did they say someone can't make it?)
Dual Appointment
Y / N
▾
Why this field exists:
Tells the specialist whether this is a one-person or two-person appointment. They adjust their presentation accordingly. If this is blank, they're guessing.

✓ Good: Y — Sarah + Mark Thompson
✗ Bad: Left blank
Specialist Role Explained
Y / N
▾
Why this field exists:
Did you explain what the specialist actually does — roof check, system design, tailored recommendations? Customers who understand this are less likely to cancel or be surprised on the day.

✓ Good: Y — explained specialist will assess roof, usage, and design system
✗ Bad: N (with no note — was it forgotten or intentional?)
Duration (60–90 min) Explained
Y / N
▾
Why this field exists:
A customer who wasn't told the session takes 60–90 minutes is more likely to cut it short or cancel. This field confirms the expectation was set.

✓ Good: Y — customer acknowledged 60–90 min, said that's fine
✗ Bad: Y (but customer said it sounded long — not noted in objections)
ZOOM & DOCUMENTS
Zoom Protocol Explained
Y / N
▾
Why this field exists:
Covers three things: link arrives 15 min before, check spam folder, test early. If the customer doesn't know to check spam and misses the link, that's a no-show.

✓ Good: Y — covered 15 min prior, spam folder, test link
✗ Bad: Y — "told them about Zoom" (which parts? All three?)
Electricity Bill & Meter Box Attached & Clear
Y / N
▾
Why this field exists:
The specialist needs both documents to prepare the system design before the appointment. If they're missing, the specialist goes in blind. This field flags whether they've been received.

✓ Good: N — requested, customer said they'll send before Thursday
✗ Bad: N (with no follow-up — were they asked? Are they coming?)
ADDITIONAL NOTES
Additional Notes / Objections
FREE TEXT
▾
Why this field exists:
This is where the real context lives. Any objection raised, any uncertainty expressed, any change to the situation — it goes here. Don't summarise; be specific.

✓ Good: "Customer mentioned husband might be late — will join 15 min into session. Customer concerned about 60–90 min duration — explained value, she said OK. Bill not received — said she'd send today."

✗ Bad: "Spoke to customer. All good." or left blank.
FILLED EXAMPLE
What a complete note looks like
CONFIRMATION CALL NOTES Confirmed Appointment Date & Time: Y
Confirmed All Decision Makers Will Be Present: Y
Dual Appointment: Y
Specialist Role Explained: Y
Duration (60–90 min) Explained: Y
Zoom Protocol Explained: Y
Electricity Bill & Meter Box Attached & Clear: N↓ see notes

Additional Notes / Objections: "Spoke with Sarah — confirmed Thurs 2PM. Mark (husband) will also attend. Explained specialist role, 60–90 min duration and Zoom link process. Customer happy. Electricity bill not yet received — Sarah said she'll send through this afternoon. Meter box photo also outstanding — reminded her. No objections raised."

💡 Note how the N on electricity bill is explained in the additional notes. Anyone reading this knows exactly what happened, what's outstanding, and what to follow up on.

GOOD VS BAD
Spot the difference.

Same call. Same outcome. Two very different notes. Read both and ask yourself: which specialist would you rather be?

❌ POOR NOTE
Confirmed Appointment: Y
Decision Makers: Y
Dual: Y
Role: Y
Duration: Y
Zoom: Y
Docs: N

Called. All good.
What does the specialist know? Nothing useful. "All good" — is the bill coming? Did anyone raise concerns? When were they confirmed? This note is useless to everyone who reads it after you.
✅ STRONG NOTE
Confirmed Appointment: Y
Decision Makers: Y — both Tom + Rachel
Dual: Y
Role: Y — specialist role explained
Duration: Y — customer acknowledged
Zoom: Y — 15min link, spam, test early
Docs: N — requested, Tom to send today

Tom confirmed Fri 11AM. Rachel (wife) attending. Tom asked about PAYS — explained $0 upfront, he seemed relieved. Bill + meter box not yet received, reminded him. No other objections.
The specialist knows: who's coming, what was discussed, what's outstanding, and that PAYS was raised. They can prepare. That's what a note is for.

FREE TEXT
▾

Partner or co-owner name. Critical for dual attendance — the specialist needs to know who else will be in the room.

Email / Phone / Address
FREE TEXT
▾
Why this field exists:
Always ask them to spell the email. Confirm the full address including suburb. The Zoom link and SMS go to these details — one wrong character means a missed link.

✓ Good: sarah.thompson@gmail.com | 0412 555 890 | 14 Coral St, Cronulla NSW 2230
✗ Bad: sarahthompson@gmail (missing domain, no suburb)
