Tagging
in Zoho
A tag sends an email to whoever you tag. Used well, it keeps jobs moving. Used loosely, it floods inboxes and pulls people off the work that matters.
🏷️ When to tag 👥 Who to tag 📋 The Blueprint ✅ Quiz
THE GOLDEN RULES
Before you tag anyone.
🎯
Tag only when attention is genuinely needed
For help, a decision, or to confirm something is done. If no one needs to act — don't tag.
1️⃣
Tag one person or one group — not five
Pick the owner. Group tags reach the right team even if one person is away. Five tags = five people's inboxes cluttered with something only one team needs to act on.
📢
Lead with the action
A clear summary, the issue, the instruction, or exactly what you need done. Don't make people guess what you want.
💾
Double-check before you save
Editing and re-saving a note re-sends the email to everyone tagged. Get it right the first time. Do not "re-send".
🚫
Don't tag for "thanks" or non-essential FYIs
We all know our efforts are appreciated. Leave a note instead of pinging the team. Save tags for when action is needed.
HOW TO TAG
Two steps.
In the Notes section of a record, type @ and start typing the person or team name. Pick the right suggestion from the list, add your clear note, and save once.

When you are tagged, you will receive an email. Click the lead or record name in it to jump straight to the record and action it.
WHO TO TAG
Follow the customer journey.

Every stage of a job has a team that owns it. Tag the stage the job is in — and it lands with the people who can act, not on a rep who is mid-sprint.

BEFORE THE APPOINTMENT
@Customer Experience
Booking, calendar management and rescheduling — before or after the appointment.
SIGNED → INSTALL-READY
@Senior Ops Team
Tech checks, desktop reviews, pre-site and technical questions. Gets the job install-ready, then hands to Fulfilment.
THE INSTALL
@Fulfilment Team
All install relations, concerns or feedback — including booking and coordinating the install itself.
AFTER INSTALLATION
@Support Team
System performance, monitoring, Wi-Fi connectivity and other system issues.
MONEY MATTERS — AT ANY STAGE

THE BLUEPRINT
Getting the sale right first.

The Blueprint lives in Zoho. It's the rep's check that the sale is correct before it moves. A clean blueprint lets Senior Ops pick a job up and run with it — no back-and-forth.

💰
Price right
Pricing correct and consistent across all documents.
📦
Stock right
Correct system and components selected for the customer.
📎
Attachments right
Signed order form, matching estimator, site photos, tech notes. Everything Senior Ops needs to proceed.
WHEN A TAG GOES BACK TO THE REP
Only two reasons.

Outside of these two reasons, the team that owns the stage sorts it — it never lands back on the rep. That's the goal.

1. To fix their own work
Incorrect paperwork — price, stock, or attachments wrong
Sold something incorrectly — rep clarifies the tech
2. A call only they can make
Change the deal, resize, or re-price
Deal at risk — customer wants to cancel or is unhappy
Customer asks for their rep by name
New opportunity, upgrade, referral, or second system
Reputation issue tied to their specific sale
BEING RESPONSIVE
One tag. One owner. One day.
⚡
RESPOND PROMPTLY
When you are tagged and action is needed, respond so other teams are not chasing you for an outcome.
📅
ONE BUSINESS DAY
Already tagged someone? Do not re-tag — give them the day. One tag, one owner, one business day.
🎯
REPS — STAY ON BOOKING
Stay on booking fresh appointments during your sprint, then clear your tags and admin once the sprint is done.
